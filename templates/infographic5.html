Chapter 3: Putting it all together

Recall that Standard Deviation is simply a measure of how spread out the values in a dataset are around the mean.

• If the standard deviation is low (relative to the mean), it means that the data points tend to cluster more closely to the mean.

• If the standard deviation is high (relative to the mean), it means that the data points are spread out over a wider range of values.
